How It Works
FROM
CREATION
TO
RESULTS.
01
Event Creation

Admin creates an event in DRAFT state. Configures challenges — setting types, scoring criteria, and datasets for Analytical challenges — then adds learning tracks if training is included. Publishes when the event is ready for registration.

02
Registration

Participants discover PUBLISHED events in the Event Catalog, enroll to create an enrollment record, and optionally join an existing team or create a new one with a nominated captain before the event goes ACTIVE.

03
Training

Participants progress through modules, engage with content — text, video, interactive — and complete exercises of quiz, coding, or analysis types. Completions are tracked automatically and contribute to overall track progress.

04
Competition

During ACTIVE hackathon events, participants submit solutions to challenges via the Submission Portal. Analytical challenges accept dataset results; Non-analytical challenges accept documents and presentations for manual review.

05
Evaluation

Judges assigned to challenges review the submission queue in the Judging Panel. They enter scores from 0 to 100 and optional written feedback. Each submission moves from PENDING to EVALUATED upon scoring.

06
Results

Live leaderboards automatically reflect scores as submissions are evaluated. Rankings visible by individual and by team. Once all challenges are scored, the event moves to COMPLETED and results are finalised.

Training Portal
STRUCTURED
LEARNING
AT SCALE.

The Training Portal is the learner-facing Workshop application for structured skill development. Built on Palantir Workshop, it presents learning tracks as progression paths — from beginner to advanced — with content loaded from Foundry datasets via contentRef. Every completion is modelled as an Ontology action, creating a persistent, queryable competency record that administrators can analyse in real time through Contour.

What is Vanyar
NOT AN LMS
WITH A
Palantir skin.

Generic learning management systems were built for generic organisations. They track completions. They report on clicks. They generate PDFs. What they cannot do — and were never designed to do — is model competency as a living, queryable object inside the same operational infrastructure where your teams actually work.

Vanyar is built natively on Palantir Foundry. Training progress is modelled as an Ontology. Actions are Foundry Actions. Analytics run in Contour and Workshop. The same infrastructure that powers your AIP deployments is the infrastructure that tracks whether your team can build on it.

Architecture
14 OBJECT
TYPES. ONE
ONTOLOGY.

Vanyar is modelled on the Aavya Ontology — a Palantir Foundry ontology on branch km/vanyar-ontology-setup at aavya.palantirfoundry.com. Every platform concept — users, organisations, events, challenges, submissions, scores, tracks, modules — exists as a typed object with defined properties and linked relationships across 22 link types.

The Ontology drives everything: Workshop applications read and write through it, Actions validate against it, Contour analytics query it, and Checkpoint audits it. This is not a database with a UI on top. It is a living operational model of your training and competition ecosystem.
